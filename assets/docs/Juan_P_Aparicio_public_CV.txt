JOB MARKET PAPER

Peacefully Demobilizing Rebels: Identities, Emotional Cues, and the FARC
with Michael Jetter and Christopher Parsons. Role: First and corresponding author; project developed from Chapters 3 and 4 of my doctoral dissertation.
Revised July 2026; earlier version: IZA Discussion Paper No. 16054; under journal review
https://www.posadaaparicio.com/assets/docs/Juan_Aparicio_Job_Market_Paper.pdf
Abstract: In the early 2000s, the Colombian government broadcast radio messages during national-team football matches encouraging FARC combatants to demobilize. We combine match dates and kick-off times with spatial and temporal variation in rain-induced radio reception in a municipality-day panel covering 2003-2016. Family-oriented messages induced more than 1,000 demobilizations, while appeals to national unity had no detectable effect. Persuasion was strongest following unexpected losses, when negative emotional cues made family identity more salient. A model combining identity salience and emotional state, together with tests using holidays and local climatic anomalies, supports this mechanism. The results show that the effectiveness of persuasion depends on the interaction between message content, identity, and emotional context.

WORKING PAPERS

The Wrong Snowball: Reintegration Signals and Demobilization in Colombia
with Verónica Abril. Working paper.
State-funded business openings by former combatants were followed by fewer nearby demobilizations, showing how visible but fragile reintegration outcomes can discourage armed-group exit.
https://www.posadaaparicio.com/assets/docs/Aparicio_Abril_Wrong_Snowball.pdf

You Speak, We Shoot: On the Unintended Consequences of Community Radios on Colombia's Violent Conflict
with Gustavo Canavire-Bacarreza and Eric Neumayer. Under journal review.
Community-radio entry was associated with increased killings by right-wing paramilitaries but not by the FARC, illustrating how participatory media can disrupt territorial power.
https://www.posadaaparicio.com/assets/docs/Aparicio_Canavire-Bacarreza_Neumayer_Community_Radios.pdf

Looks Expensive Until It Doesn't: The Timing of Pain-and-Palliative Care in Colombia
with Diana Echavarria. Working paper.
Using Colombia's national health claims and risk-set matching, earlier specialist access is associated with roughly 25 percent lower recorded consultation and procedure values than delayed access; a simple prior-spending rule captures most of the value of machine-learning targeting.
https://www.posadaaparicio.com/assets/docs/Aparicio_Echavarria_Palliative_Care.pdf
