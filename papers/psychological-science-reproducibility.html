Mass Reproducibility in Psychological Science

A 63-team project audits reproducibility, reporting errors, and preregistration practices in Psychological Science.

Back to research
Research brief

Question and contribution.

This collaboration with Psychological Science mobilizes 63 independent groups to audit materials availability, coding and preregistration issues, and computational reproducibility. It creates a pre-reform benchmark for how often published evidence can be reproduced from available data and code.

Paper

Abstract.

In a collaboration with Psychological Science, 63 independent groups assess data and code availability, identify coding and preregistration issues, and computationally reproduce published results. The project establishes a pre-reform baseline for reproducibility and reporting practices in the journal.
