Job Market Paper

Peacefully Demobilizing Rebels: Identity, Emotional Cues, and the FARC

With Michael Jetter and Christopher Parsons

Using match timing and rain-induced variation in radio reception, we show that family-oriented messages broadcast during national-team football matches induced more than 1,000 FARC demobilizations. Appeals to national unity had no detectable effect, and persuasion was strongest after unexpected losses made family identity more salient.

Profile

Two distinct strands: political economy and research credibility.

My political-economy research studies conflict, media, crime, and institutions, primarily in Colombia. It asks how information, identity, and public policy change the choices of armed actors and communities.

Separately, my research on credibility studies whether empirical findings reproduce and how research teams work with AI. At the Institute for Replication, I design and coordinate multi-team replication studies; for the PNAS AI Games project, I helped design and conduct the research, organize replication games, and write the paper.
